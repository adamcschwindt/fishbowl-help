Your two questions
Discounts How do I record a discount without manually calculating it?
Short answer: Use Fishbowl's built-in Discount button. Type a percent (%) or a dollar amount and Fishbowl does the math for you — you never type the calculated number.
  1. Open the sales order and add your product line item(s) as usual.
  2. Click the Discount button to the right of the item table on the sales order.
  3. Enter the discount as a percentage (e.g. 10%) or a fixed dollar amount. Fishbowl recalculates the line automatically.
  4. To discount one line: apply it to that line. For several lines or the whole order: add a Subtotal row first, then put the discount right below it.

Behind the scenes each line is figured as Unit Price × Quantity − Discount, so the order total updates live.

Time-saver: Build discounts you use often (e.g. “10% Contractor”) once in the Discount module under the Sales group, then apply with one click.

Source: Fishbowl — Discount · Fishbowl — Sales Order

QuickBooks I reconciled the invoice in Fishbowl but can't see it in QuickBooks. Why?
Short answer: Reconciling in Fishbowl only queues the invoice. Nothing reaches QuickBooks until you run the Export from Fishbowl's Accounting Integration module.
  1. Confirm the sales order is fully shipped/fulfilled in Fishbowl — shipping is what creates the invoice and cost-of-goods entry.
  2. Open the Accounting Integration module (in the Accounting group).
  3. If you use QuickBooks Desktop, open your company file so it can receive the data.
  4. Click the Export button in the toolbar.
  5. Choose Standard Export — sends everything fulfilled since the last successful export. A progress box lists each item.

One shipped sales order becomes an Invoice in QuickBooks (the sale) plus a Journal Entry (cost of goods sold).

If it's still missing after exporting

  • The export hasn't been run. Most common cause — run a Standard Export.
  • It was “Marked as Posted.” That skips transactions and they cannot be re-exported.
  • Account mapping. Check the Fishbowl→QuickBooks account mappings.
  • It was edited directly in QuickBooks. Don't — change it in Fishbowl and re-export, or the two won't match.

Receiving & Purchase Orders

  1. Open Fishbowl and go to the Purchase Order module.
  2. Locate the correct PO. Confirm vendor, items, quantities, costs, and expected receipt.
  3. Issue the PO if it hasn't been issued yet.
  4. Go to Receiving from the PO workflow.
  5. Select the items being received and enter the actual quantity received.
  6. Select the correct receiving location.
  7. Finish the receipt, then confirm inventory now shows in the correct location.
Do not cycle count new vendor receipts unless instructed — PO receiving preserves the purchasing trail.

SOP How do I reconcile a receipt against the vendor bill (with freight / landed cost)?

Do this after receiving inventory, so item cost is accurate before the accounting export.

  1. Open the received order in the Receiving module and click Reconcile.
  2. Review each unreconciled received line. Uncheck any item that shouldn't be reconciled yet.
  3. Enter the vendor bill details.
  4. Add freight, shipping, duties, fees, or other extra costs if needed.
  5. Land those costs across the received items when the fees should become part of item cost.
  6. Confirm received quantity, item cost, and landed-cost allocation.
  7. Finish the reconcile. The receipt is now ready for QuickBooks export.

SOP What if I only received part of the order, or items are over/short/damaged?

Partial receipt

  • Receive only the quantity physically received; leave the rest open.
  • Reconcile only the received items that match the vendor bill.
  • Receive and reconcile the remaining quantities later.

Over, short, or damaged

  • Compare packing slip, vendor bill, and physical count.
  • Receive only usable inventory; hold, reject, or document damaged inventory.
  • Leave missing items open if still expected.
  • Reconcile only what should be financially recognized.

Items & Bills of Materials (-RG Raw Goods)
SOP What's the difference between a finished good and a -RG raw good?
  • Finished Good — the sellable item (eCommerce, Amazon, Sales Orders, Transfer Orders). Does not end in -RG, and should not keep the UPC once the -RG exists.
  • Raw Good (-RG) — the inventory component used on the BOM and consumed during manufacturing. Ends in -RG and keeps the UPC for receiving and barcode scanning.

Example: finished good PS-B-BOMB-TALL → raw good PS-B-BOMB-TALL-RG.

Source: Remington Supply Fishbowl SOP · Fishbowl — Part

SOP How do I create a missing -RG raw good item?
Always duplicate the finished good — never build the raw good by hand. Duplication copies inventory settings, expiration tracking, and other config, saving time and avoiding errors.
  1. Go to Part, search for and open the finished good. Verify it's the correct item.
  2. Click Duplicate, then OK. Fishbowl creates “Copy Of [Part Number]”.
  3. Remove “Copy Of” from the part number and add -RG to the end.
  4. Review/update the description to mark it as the raw good version.
  5. Verify tracking settings copied correctly, then save the new -RG item.

Move the UPC to the -RG item

  1. Verify the UPC copied to the new -RG item; save it with the UPC intact.
  2. Open the original finished good, remove the UPC, and save.
  3. Confirm the UPC now lives only on the -RG item (finished good = blank).
Duplicate one item at a time — Fishbowl only duplicates the item currently open. If you duplicate the wrong item, delete the copy and start over with the correct finished good.

If an error references a missing -RG part: copy that part number, remove the -RG suffix, find & open the matching finished good, duplicate it, re-add -RG, and save.

Source: Remington Supply Fishbowl SOP · Fishbowl — Part

SOP How do I create or update a BOM for a finished good?

A BOM lets Fishbowl consume the raw good and create the finished good during manufacturing.

Create a new BOM

  1. Open the Bill of Materials module and click New BOM.
  2. Name it using the finished-good part number (or approved naming convention).
  3. Add the finished good as the Output Item and set the output quantity.
  4. Add the matching -RG item as an Input Component with its required quantity.
  5. Add any packaging, prep materials, labels, boxes, or other tracked components.
  6. Review all quantities and units of measure, then save and confirm the BOM is active.

Add a missing -RG to an existing BOM

  1. Open the existing BOM and click Edit.
  2. Add the matching -RG item as a component with its quantity.
  3. Review prep material and box components, save, and verify it's available for Manufacture Orders.

Manufacturing & Transfers to Amazon
SOP Weekly production → Amazon FBA: what's the current workflow and who does what?
The recurring weekly flow: Becca creates the MOs; the warehouse builds them and accumulates finished goods onto one weekly Transfer Order; at week's end the warehouse processes & reconciles the TO to Amazon FBA Inbound; a script then moves it into sellable Amazon FBA once Amazon confirms receipt.

The flow at a glance

MO per SKU (Becca) → warehouse completes each MO → finished items added to the week's TO → warehouse processes the TO (Remington warehouse → FBA Inbound) → warehouse reconciles (+ processing fee + freight, + FBA shipment ID in the note) → script moves FBA Inbound → Amazon FBA on receipt.

Who does what

  • Create MOs (one per SKU, with due date) — Becca (Adam accountable).
  • Produce finished goods / complete MOs — Warehouse team.
  • Add finished items to the weekly TO — Warehouse team.
  • Process & reconcile the weekly TO, enter the FBA shipment ID — Warehouse team (Vicky consulted on fees/freight).
  • FBA Inbound → Amazon FBA on receipt — automated script, no manual step.
  • Backorder report auto-export & review — Adam sets up; reviewer TBD.

Source: Remington Supply — Manufacturing Order & Transfer Order SOP (current state, effective 2026-07-07)

SOP MO → TO → FBA, step by step (the 8 steps)
  1. Create Manufacturing OrdersBecca — one MO per SKU, each with a due date, per the week's production schedule.
  2. Warehouse processes each MOWarehouse — produce the finished goods against each MO by its due date.
  3. Add finished items to the Transfer OrderWarehouse — immediately as each MO completes (continuously through the week, not just at the end). Add them onto that week's running TO.
  4. Process the weekly TO to Amazon FBAWarehouse — once the week's done, process the accumulated TO; it ships from the Remington warehouse to the Amazon FBA Inbound location.
  5. Reconcile the TOWarehouse — as soon as it's processed (don't wait for Amazon receipt), add the inbound processing fee and freight so you capture true landed cost at the point of send.
  6. Enter the Amazon FBA shipment IDWarehouse — in the same sitting, put the FBA shipment ID into the TO's note field, tying the Fishbowl TO to the Amazon shipment.
  7. Automated move: FBA Inbound → Amazon FBAAutomatic — the script detects when Amazon receives the shipment and moves the inventory into Amazon FBA (sellable). No manual action under normal operation.
  8. Backorder report monitoringScheduled — a recurring auto-export of the Backorder report runs so anything slipping through gets caught and routed. Cadence TBD (daily / every other day recommended).

SOP MO / TO exceptions and edge cases (what to do when it's not clean)?
  • MO due date missed — warehouse flags Becca; finished items still go onto the TO whenever the MO completes. If that week's TO already shipped, they roll into next week's.
  • Script didn't detect receipt / stuck in FBA Inbound — check Amazon Seller Central to confirm actual receipt before assuming the script failed. If Amazon shows received but Fishbowl hasn't moved it, escalate to Adam.
  • Fees not known at reconciliation — use your best estimate for processing fee/freight, then true up if the actual invoice differs materially.
  • BOM / “Available to Build” issue blocks an MO — don't force it; that's a prerequisite issue. Escalate to Becca/Adam for a BOM fix before continuing that SKU.
  • FBA shipment ID not available yet — reconcile the fee/freight as normal and add the shipment ID to the note as soon as it's issued; don't leave the note blank.
  • Backorder auto-export fails — treat as a system issue, not just a missed report; flag Adam so the schedule/delivery is fixed (a silent failure defeats the purpose).

SOP How do I plan and create a Manufacture Order (MO)?

Weekly planning

  • Review Amazon inventory and current warehouse inventory.
  • Identify what needs to be built; confirm BOMs are accurate.
  • Review cleanup items (FNSKU naming, labor lines, non-inventory components).
  • Decide what finished goods will be produced this week.

Create the MO

  1. Go to the Manufacture Order module and create a new MO.
  2. Name it using the weekly transfer date (or agreed format).
  3. Add all planned finished goods and confirm quantities.
  4. Save, then Issue the MO.

Print & build

  1. After issuing, print the MO Traveler, Work Order Travelers, and Pick Tickets.
  2. Group pick tickets with the matching traveler and give the packet to production.
  3. Start the work order, pick components, complete the build, and record completed quantities.
  4. Finish the work order and confirm finished-goods inventory is available.

SOP FBA Transfer Order: what has to be true before I start?

Applies to every FBA shipment leaving Remington Warehouse. Amazon is the source of truth for anything that has left the building.

  • All finished goods must exist in Fishbowl first — complete/receive the MO before picking. If a lot is missing, the MO wasn't finished — fix the MO; don't cycle-count product into existence.
  • Every part on the shipment needs a non-zero weight on its Part record (check the TALL-jar SKUs — several were missing weights). Zero-weight parts get $0 of the freight allocation and skew everyone else's landed cost.

Source: Remington Supply — FBA Transfer Order SOP (Warehouse → Amazon)

SOP How do I create the FBA Transfer Order (destination + fast CSV method)?
Destination location group = “Amazon FBA Inbound”, NOT “Amazon FBA.” Inbound is the staging bucket; the automation moves stock to Amazon FBA when Amazon actually receives it.

Fast way to build a big TO

  1. On the Import/Export screen, export an existing TO (“Transfer Order” type).
  2. Edit the CSV: header row TO + one Item row per SKU, Status 10 = Entered.
  3. Re-import.

Never guess CSV formats — always start from a Fishbowl export.

SOP Receive & reconcile the TO for true landed cost (do NOT skip)

Receive

In Receiving, change the receive location from the default “Receiving” bin to “Amazon FBA Inbound” (the main bin). The automation only drains the main bin — stock left in the Receiving sub-bin is invisible to it.

Reconcile (landed cost)

  1. Receiving module → open the receipt → Reconcile tab.
  2. Add two cost lines from the shipment in Seller Central: Inbound placement service fees and Amazon partnered carrier cost.
  3. Distribution method = Weight → save. This spreads freight into each SKU's unit cost so margin reports carry true landed cost.
  4. Put the FBA Shipment ID (e.g. FBA19HZDX8C7) in the TO note field — it's the match key for reconciliation and the receiving automation.

Source: Remington Supply — FBA Transfer Order SOP (Warehouse → Amazon)

SOP FBA Transfer Order: what should I NEVER do?
  • ❌ Never do a bulk “Inventory quantities import” against the Amazon location groups. A 6/18–6/19 import fabricated ~19,000 phantom units and took a full-day reconciliation to unwind. Amazon locations are owned by the automation + the daily 3:05 PM drift check.
  • ❌ Don't use Move Inventory imports as a substitute for the TO flow when freight needs to be capitalized — moves carry cost as-is, with no reconcile hook.
  • ❌ Don't receive into the default “Receiving” sub-bin of the Amazon groups.

After receive, stock sits in Amazon FBA Inbound until Amazon checks it in; the auto-move then shifts it to Amazon FBA automatically. If Fishbowl and Amazon disagree, the daily drift check emails Adam.

SOP Clear Conscience daily workflow, start to finish (what's automatic vs. manual)?

In the order it actually happens:

  1. PO email arrivesAutomatic — Clear Conscience's order lands in the shared inbox. The system checks every couple of minutes; no one needs to watch for it.
  2. Order logged & confirmedAutomatic — the order is read, matched to the right product (12oz or 3oz case), added as a new Orders-tab row, and an itemized confirmation email goes to Clear Conscience.SKUs that don't match are flagged instead of confirmed — see exceptions.
  3. Review the orderManual — click Open email on that row to see the original PO, and confirm the parsed line matches it.
  4. Mark it verifiedManual — type y in the Verify(y) column. That's the signal it's cleared to enter Fishbowl.
  5. Order flows into FishbowlAutomatic — within about a minute it's created as a real sales order numbered CC-<PO number>. Status moves New → Queued.
  6. Pick, pack, shipManual — handle it like any other Fishbowl sales order. Quirk: Fishbowl shows the carrier as Will Call — that's just how FedEx is set up here, not an instruction. These still ship FedEx Ground.
  7. Tracking flows backAutomatic — once you mark it Shipped, the FedEx tracking number(s) and ship date populate the row (usually within minutes). Status moves to Imported and the row sorts to the bottom.
  8. FedEx picks upManual — the truck comes weekly, usually Wednesday. A tracking number does not mean it's been picked up — Fishbowl only knows it's labeled. When the truck takes the boxes, enter today's date in the “Last Pickup” box; this resets the running case count for the next pickup.

SOP Clear Conscience Orders tab — what does each column mean?

Columns can be reordered freely for your own view — nothing depends on where a column sits, only on its name.

  • Verify(y) — type y once you've reviewed the order. Nothing downstream happens until this is set.
  • Status — New (not yet verified), Queued (verified, waiting to appear in Fishbowl), Imported (confirmed in Fishbowl; sorts to the bottom).
  • OrderEmail — “Open email” links back to the original PO for review.
  • PO — the customer's purchase order number, taken verbatim from their email.
  • 12oz Cases / 3oz Cases — plain case counts per SKU; sum them for a case total across any rows.
  • Tracking — FedEx tracking number(s), filled in automatically once the order ships.
  • Shipped Date — the date Fishbowl marked it Shipped — not the date FedEx picked it up.
  • Awaiting FedEx pickup (top right) — running case total shipped since the last pickup date you logged; resets to zero each time you update “Last Pickup.”

SOP Clear Conscience exceptions: order changes, damaged cases, unknown products?

Anything outside a clean PO → clean shipment gets handled by hand, on purpose.

Order change or cancellation

A reply to an existing PO thread doesn't match the automated format, so it's never silently processed — it's flagged and an alert goes to the office automatically. No warehouse action needed; just don't assume a reply email was acted on until someone confirms it.

Damaged case / replacement

Don't touch the original order — it's an accurate record of what actually shipped. Instead:

  1. Create a small, separate sales order in Fishbowl for just the replacement case(s), priced at $0 with no processing fee — it's a make-good, not a sale.
  2. If you want it visible on the shared sheet, add one Orders-tab row using that same order number in the PO column — tracking populates on its own once you ship it.
  3. Send a short, personal reply to the customer rather than the automated confirmation template — it isn't worded for a replacement.

Line item doesn't match a known product

The order is logged but not auto-confirmed to the customer — it's flagged for review. Resolve the mismatch, then verify it manually once it's correct.

QuickBooks Standard Export vs. Repost vs. Mark as Posted?
  • Standard Export — the everyday option. Sends everything fulfilled/completed since the last successful export.
  • Repost — re-sends all entries in a date range. Recovery only; can create duplicates if misused.
  • Mark as Posted — flags transactions as done without sending them, and they can't be re-exported. Only on an accountant's instruction.

Source: Fishbowl — QuickBooks

QuickBooks Why don't my Fishbowl and QuickBooks numbers match?

Usual culprits, most common first:

  • Account mapping is off. Always start here.
  • Someone edited/deleted a transaction in QuickBooks instead of fixing it in Fishbowl. Fix in Fishbowl and re-export.
  • Duplicate entries from creating inventory transactions directly in QuickBooks.
  • Back-dated or forward-dated transactions throw off point-in-time reconciliation.
  • The systems weren't reconciled at go-live, so a gap carries forward.

Golden rule: record and change everything in Fishbowl, then export to QuickBooks.

Source: Top 10 reasons FB & QB don't match

QuickBooks Can I just fix a sales order or invoice directly in QuickBooks?
No. Fishbowl is the system of record for anything inventory-related. Change it in QuickBooks and the accounts drift apart.

Correct it in Fishbowl, then run the export again so QuickBooks matches.
